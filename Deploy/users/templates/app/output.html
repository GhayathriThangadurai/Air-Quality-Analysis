Good (0-50)
Description:

Air quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.

Symptoms:
  • No adverse health effects expected for the general population.
Causes:
  • Low levels of air pollutants due to favorable weather conditions and low emissions.
  • Effective air quality management policies and practices.
Prevention:
  • No specific actions needed.
Potential Diseases:
  • No significant risk of diseases due to air pollution at this level.
{% endif %} {% if predict == 'Moderate' %}
Moderate (51-100)
Description:

Air quality is acceptable; however, some pollutants may pose a moderate health concern for a small number of people who are sensitive to air pollution.

Symptoms:
  • People with respiratory or heart conditions may experience mild symptoms.
  • Sensitive individuals may experience mild irritation of eyes, nose, and throat.
Causes:
  • Moderate levels of pollutants from vehicle emissions, industrial activities, or natural sources.
  • Increased use of fossil fuels and industrial emissions.
Prevention:
  • Sensitive individuals should limit prolonged outdoor exertion.
Potential Diseases:
  • Possible aggravation of respiratory conditions such as asthma.
{% endif %} {% if predict == 'Satisfactory' %}
Satisfactory (101-150)
Description:

Air quality is satisfactory, but there may be a moderate health concern for a very small number of people who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.

Symptoms:
  • Sensitive individuals may experience respiratory symptoms.
Causes:
  • Pollutants from vehicle emissions, industrial activities, and natural sources.
  • Adverse weather conditions.
Prevention:
  • Sensitive individuals should limit outdoor activities.
Potential Diseases:
  • Minor risk of aggravating existing respiratory conditions.
{% endif %} {% if predict == 'Poor' %}
Poor (151-200)
Description:

Air quality is unhealthy for sensitive groups. The general public is not likely to be affected.

Symptoms:
  • Sensitive individuals may experience more serious health effects.
  • Children, elderly, and individuals with pre-existing health conditions may experience aggravated symptoms.
Causes:
  • Higher levels of pollutants from traffic, industrial emissions, and other sources.
  • Adverse weather conditions trapping pollutants close to the ground.
Prevention:
  • Sensitive groups should reduce prolonged or heavy exertion outdoors.
Potential Diseases:
  • Risk of exacerbation of respiratory and cardiovascular conditions.
{% endif %} {% if predict == 'Very Poor' %}
Very Poor (201-300)
Description:

Health alert: everyone may experience more serious health effects.

Symptoms:
  • Increased likelihood of adverse health effects for everyone.
  • Sensitive groups may experience severe symptoms such as asthma attacks and cardiovascular issues.
Causes:
  • Very high levels of pollutants from sources like heavy traffic, industrial activities, and natural events.
  • Stagnant air conditions exacerbating pollution levels.
Prevention:
  • Everyone should avoid all outdoor exertion. Sensitive groups should remain indoors and keep activity levels low.
Potential Diseases:
  • High risk of severe respiratory and cardiovascular diseases.
{% endif %} {% if predict == 'Severe' %}
Severe (301-500)
Description:

Health warnings of emergency conditions: the entire population is more likely to be affected.

Symptoms:
  • Serious risk of respiratory and cardiovascular effects for the entire population.
  • Severe health effects for sensitive groups, including respiratory and heart conditions.
Causes:
  • Extremely high levels of pollutants from sources such as wildfires, industrial accidents, and severe traffic congestion.
  • Extremely adverse weather conditions trapping pollutants.
Prevention:
  • Everyone should avoid all outdoor activity. Sensitive groups should remain indoors with windows and doors closed.
  • Use air purifiers to reduce indoor pollution levels.
Potential Diseases:
  • High risk of severe respiratory and cardiovascular diseases.
  • Increased incidence of respiratory infections and exacerbation of chronic diseases.
